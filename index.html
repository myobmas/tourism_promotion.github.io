Kavimane, Kuppalli

Kavimane is the ancestral home of celebrated Kannada poet Kuvempu, located in the lush green hills of Kuppalli, Karnataka. Built in traditional Malnad architectural style, the house is now a museum preserving Kuvempu’s life, achievements, and literary legacy. It's a peaceful cultural retreat for literature lovers and students alike.

  • 📍 Address: Kavimane, Kuppalli, Thirthahalli Taluk, Shivamogga District, Karnataka 577424
  • 🕒 Timings: 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~80 km (2 hrs approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹100 – ₹150 (by bus)
  • 💰 Travel Route from Shimoga: Travel by Thirthahalli bus. Take a bus to koppa and get down near kuppalli
  • 🎟️ Entry Fees: ₹10

Kavaledurga Fort, Thirthahalli

Kavaledurga Fort is a beautiful hilltop fort located near Thirthahalli in Shivamogga district. Surrounded by dense forests and valleys, the fort offers a moderate trek and rewards visitors with panoramic views of the Western Ghats. Built in the 9th century and later strengthened by Shivappa Nayaka, the ruins include temples, water tanks, and strong fort walls.

  • 📍 Address: Kavaledurga Fort, near Thirthahalli, Shivamogga District, Karnataka 577432
  • 🕒 Timings: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM (Open all days)
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~80 km (1.5 hrs approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹100 – ₹200 (by local transport or vehicle)
  • 🎟️ Entry Fees: Free

Udupi Sri Krishna Matha

Udupi Sri Krishna Matha is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna located in Udupi, Karnataka. The temple is known for its unique way of worshipping through the Navagraha window and its vibrant spiritual atmosphere. It is one of the major pilgrimage destinations for devotees of Lord Krishna.

  • 📍 Address: Car Street, Udupi, Karnataka 576101
  • 🕒 Timings: 5:00 AM – 9:00 PM (Daily)
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~180 km (4–5 hrs approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹250 – ₹400 (bus)
  • 🎟️ Entry Fees: Free

Bale Padmavathi Sagara

Bale Padmavathi Sagara, is a serene temple surrounded by nature and greenery. It is a lesser-known peaceful spot ideal for relaxation, nature photography, and enjoying the calm waters. The area is not commercialized, making it a hidden gem near Shimoga.

  • 🕒 Timings: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~60 km (1 hr approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹50 – ₹100

Sakrebailu Elephant Camp

Sakrebailu Elephant Camp is a training and rehabilitation center for elephants, located on the banks of the Tunga River. It offers a chance to see elephants being fed, bathed, and trained in a natural environment. A popular spot for animal lovers and families visiting Shimoga.

  • 📍 Address: SH 57, Gajanur, Shivamogga, Karnataka 577202
  • 🕒 Timings: 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM (Visit in the morning only)
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~14 km (30 min approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹30 – ₹60
  • 🎟️ Entry Fees: ₹30 for adults, ₹10 for children, extra cost to spend time with elephants

Siganduru

Sigandur or Sigandooru is a village near Tumari, located in the Indian state of Karnataka. The village is known for the Sigandur Chowdeshwari Temple, a Hindu pilgrimage site that attracts hundreds daily. Shree Chowdeshwari Temple is named Sigandur. Shree Devi kshethra was built in the 18th century. Sigandur kshethra is near Tumari, 42 km from Sagar town. Mother Choudeshwari is called “Sigandureshwari”.

  • 📍 Address: Shree Sigandur Chowdeshwari Temple, Village - Kalasavalli, Post - Tumari, Taluk - Sagara,
  • 🕒 Timings: 7:30 AM – 7:30 PM
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~142 km (130 min approx.)
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹200 – ₹300

Balligavi

Balligavi a town in Shikaripura taluk Shivamogga district of Karnataka state, India, is today known as Belagami or Balagame. Its ancient names are Baligrama, Dakshina Kedara, Valliggame and Valligrame. Dakshina Kedara means Kedarnath of the South. A place of antiquity, it is known for its ancient monuments. Balligavi is located 72 km from Shivamogga city and 21 km from Shikaripura town and 2.3 km from Shiralakoppa in Shikaripura taluk. Archaeologically, Balligavi dated from the Satavahana-Kadamba era and the Chaturmukha linga (four faced linga) here is in that style.The area came under the Banavasi province of the Kadamba Dynasty in 4-5th century AD.

  • 📍 Address: Balligavi, Shivamogga District, Shikaripura taluk, Karnataka, India,
  • 🕒 Timings: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • 🚗 Distance from Shimoga: ~Approximately 72 km
  • 💰 Travel Cost from Shimoga: ₹230 – ₹300
